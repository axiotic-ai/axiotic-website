Research Lab

Open Research on Efficient AI Systems

The Axiotic Lab studies efficient, multimodal, embodied learning through completely open models, datasets, and training recipes.

We treat transparency as a design principle: no black boxes, clear evaluations, and research that anyone can build on.

Research Thesis & Pillars

We believe current AI is plateauing on generalization and grounded understanding. Breakthroughs will come from new tasks and mechanisms, not just bigger models.

1. Improved Learning Tasks

Beyond next‑token prediction: masking, N‑step forecasting, and JEPA‑style objectives that force long‑horizon understanding.

2. Multimodal Learning

Early‑fusion representation learning across text, vision, audio, and interaction, with careful evaluation design.

3. Memory & Conditional Recursion

Architectures with explicit memory and adaptive, data‑conditional recurrence to support deeper reasoning.

4. Hierarchical Attention & Infinite Context

Variable‑resolution attention and feature reuse to enable near infinite context without brute‑force scaling.

5. Layer‑Wide Attention

Real‑time access across layers so models can route information freely instead of strictly layer‑by‑layer.

6. AI‑Assisted Data Loops

Data improvement loops where better models generate better data, with transparent data cards and licensing.

7. Embodied Intelligence

Simulation‑first learning (Isaac, Cosmos, etc.) and sim‑to‑real protocols for interaction‑driven learning.

8. Evolution & Nature‑Inspired Learning

Evolutionary curricula, adaptive augmentations, and information‑theoretic objectives as a first‑class focus.

Open Science Commitments

We release code, models, and datasets as openly as licensing allows, with clear documentation and ablation‑first writeups. The lab exists to push the field forward, not to hide breakthroughs behind proprietary walls.
